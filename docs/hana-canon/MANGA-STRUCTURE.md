# Hana Manga — Technical & Publishing Structure

**Series:** Agent Hana Chronicles: The Learning Blade  
**Format:** Monthly manga serialization (digital-first)  
**Issues:** 12-issue complete arc (with potential for seasonal expansions)  
**Print:** Collected volumes at arc completion  
**Reading Direction:** Right-to-left (RTL) — authentic manga format  
**Target Age:** 21+ (enforced via age gate in OS, honor system in print)  
**Availability:** Free with learning progression, available in OS after mastery thresholds

## 2. ISSUE SPECIFICATIONS (TECHNICAL)

### Page Count & Layout
```
Standard Issue:
- Cover (front, back, inside spreads): 4 pages
- Story: 30-40 pages (typical: 34)
- Back matter: 2-4 pages (author notes, character glossary, cultural explainer)
- Total: 36-44 pages per issue

Panel Structure:
- Right-to-left reading (standard manga)
- Average 4-6 panels per page
- Splash pages (1-2 per issue) for emotional beats
- Double-page spreads (1-2 per issue) for climactic moments
```

### Artwork Specifications
- **Resolution:** 1200 DPI (print quality)
- **Color:** 
  - Cover: Full color + metallic accents
  - Interior: Black & white line art with screentone shading
  - Special panels: Limited color (see below)
- **Format:** Print-ready CMYK
- **File format:** TIFF (master), PDF (for digital)
- **Dimensions:** 7" × 10.5" (A5 equivalent; standard manga size)

### Digital vs. Print
| Element | Digital | Print |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Distribution | OS, Web reader, Webtoon | Physical bookstore, Amazon, Direct |
| Color | Full color option available | B&W + screentone |
| Reading App | Custom AFROMATIONS reader (RTL native) | Physical page turn, RTL binding |
| Pricing | Free with learning (premium tier gets early access) | ¥680/volume (approx $5 USD) |
| Resolution | Mobile-optimized (web + app) | 1200 DPI print |
| Annotations | Clickable furigana, glossary, audio pronunciation | Print appendix with pronunciation guide |

## 3. VISUAL DESIGN STANDARDS

### Color Palette
- **Primary color:** Indigo (#2d3561) — dominates Hana's aesthetic
- **Accent:** Crimson (#c41e1e) — emotional beats, important moments
- **Highlight:** Gold (#d4a017) — spiritual/cultural moments
- **Neutral:** Black (#0a0a0a), White (#f5f0e8), Grey tones
- **Flashback/Dream:** Sepia or muted blue tones

### Character Design Sheet
**Hana:**
- Consistent across all issues
- Small visual evolution: Hair slightly longer by Issue 12 (shows time passage)
- Expressions: Range from neutral → slight smile → uncertainty → peace
- Proportions: Realistic (not "perfect anime"); strong but grounded

**Learner Character(s):**
- Design shows growth (clothing, posture, confidence)
- By Issue 12, noticeably more relaxed, centered
- Expressions evolve from confusion → determination → understanding → humility

**Supporting Characters:**
- Other learners: Diverse, realistic, brief appearances
- Native Japanese characters: Treated with respect, not caricature
- Hana's digital manifestation: Consistent but abstract (fractals, light, geometry)

### Font & Typography
- **Dialogue (English):** Clean sans-serif (similar to Sora)
- **Dialogue (Japanese):** Authentic Japanese fonts, size large enough to read
- **Narration:** Serif font (slightly formal, reflective)
- **Sound effects (SFX):** Integrated Japanese onomatopoeia, not translated to English cuteness
- **Example:** 
  - ✅ 「そうだね」(that's right) — read as part of story
  - ❌ *THUMP* written in comic sans — cultural loss

### Background & Environment
- **Tokyo (Issues 1-4, 8-9):** Detailed modern architecture, contrast with small traditional temples
- **Kyoto (Issues 5-7, 10):** Detailed period-accurate temples, gardens, traditional elements
- **Rural (Issue 6, 11):** Atmospheric; mist, natural light, minimal structures
- **Detail level:** Backgrounds detailed enough to support story, not so busy as to distract

## 5. CONTENT STANDARDS & SENSITIVITIES

### What We Show Explicitly
- Hana's warrior aesthetic (swords, armor) — not violence, not sexualization
- Learner struggling, failing, recovering — authentic struggle
- Cultural practice (shrine etiquette, tea ceremony) — respectful documentation
- Doubt and vulnerability (Hana, learner) — humanity

### What We Don't Show
- Gratuitous violence
- Sexualization of Hana or learners
- Stereotypes or caricatures
- Fantasy Japan (not real)
- Western gaze interpretation of Japanese culture

### Cultural Sensitivity Review
Each issue reviewed by:
- Japanese language consultant (fluency + idioms)
- Japanese cultural specialist (etiquette, accuracy)
- Sensitivity reader (representation of Black characters, disability if present)
- Manga professional (format, pacing, art standards)

**Review process:** 2-week window per issue, feedback integrated before publication

## 6. PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### Timeline per Issue (4-week cycle)
```
Week 1: Script finalization, storyboard layouts
Week 2: Pencil art, background sketches
Week 3: Inking, screentone, finalization
Week 4: Lettering, color (covers), proofread, quality check
Publication: Ready for release
```

### Team Roles
- **Mangaka (Artist/Writer):** Visual storytelling, character consistency, pacing
- **Editor:** Story flow, cultural accuracy, publication standards
- **Letterer:** Japanese/English text placement, sound effects
- **Color Artist:** Cover design, special effects, digital color (covers)
- **Cultural Advisor:** Historical, linguistic, contemporary accuracy
- **QA/Proofreader:** Spelling, typos, layout issues

### Tools & File Management
- **Art software:** Clip Studio Paint (industry standard for manga)
- **Asset management:** AFROMATIONS studio shared drive (organized by issue, version control)
- **Proofs:** PDF proofing with color-accurate monitor, print test runs monthly
- **Archival:** All original files preserved; layered PSDs kept for future editions/adaptations

## 7. READER EXPERIENCE IN OS

### Integration with Learning Engine

When learner completes prerequisite lessons for an issue:
1. **Unlock notification:** "You've mastered [Domain]. New story unlocked."
2. **Story access:** Issue appears in library with teaser image
3. **Guided reading:** First read-through offers annotations:
   - Kanji readings (furigana toggle)
   - Vocabulary definitions (click-to-expand)
   - Cultural context sidebar
4. **Reflection prompt:** After reading, learner answers question: "What did Hana teach here about Japanese culture?"
5. **Replay option:** Unlimited re-reads without annotations after first playthrough
6. **Discussion:** Community forum for each issue (peer discussion, questions)

### Progression Model
```
Unlock triggers by domain mastery:

Issue 1 → Vocabulary N5 achieved
Issue 2 → Grammar N5 achieved
Issue 3 → Culture: Shinto Concepts mastered
Issue 4 → Listening N4 attempted (at least 70% score)
Issue 5 → Complete 10+ lessons in "culture" domain
Issue 6 → Shrine etiquette mini-lesson completed
Issue 7 → Assessment score 80%+ in philosophy domain
Issue 8 → Reach 30 hours of study time
Issue 9 → Help another learner progress (community milestone)
Issue 10 → 50+ words in personal wiki
Issue 11 → Advanced mastery in 3 domains
Issue 12 → Reach "fluent" reading level OR completion of full curriculum
```
